Poseidon in Lore Olympus:

A God of the Seas and Earthquakes Poseidon, the god of the seas, earthquakes, and horses in Lore Olympus, is a complex and powerful figure. He is often portrayed as a brooding and solitary figure, deeply connected to the vastness of the ocean.
A Byronic Hero: Remender's Poseidon is a Byronic hero, a figure characterized by brooding intensity, a rebellious spirit, and a tragic flaw. He is haunted by his past and struggles with his emotions, often clashing with Zeus and other Olympians.
A God of the Seas: As the god of the seas, Poseidon commands immense power over the oceans. He can summon storms, create tidal waves, and control marine creatures. His connection to the sea is profound, and he often finds solace in its vastness.
A Complex Relationship with Zeus: Poseidon's relationship with Zeus is often fraught with tension and rivalry. While they are brothers, their differing domains and personalities frequently lead to conflict. Poseidon is often seen as a rebellious figure, challenging Zeus's authority and seeking to assert his own power.
A Tragic Figure: Despite his immense power, Poseidon is a tragic figure. He is haunted by his past, plagued by insecurities, and often feels misunderstood. His love for Athena, the goddess of wisdom and warfare, is a source of both joy and pain.
